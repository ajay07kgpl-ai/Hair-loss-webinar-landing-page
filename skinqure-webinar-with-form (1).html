Myths vs Facts

Common Hair Loss Myths — Busted

Don't let misinformation delay your treatment. Here's the medical truth:

❌ The Myth
✅ The Medical Fact
Hair loss only happens due to genetics
Hormones, stress, diet, thyroid, and scalp infections are equally common causes of hair loss
Oiling your scalp daily stops hair fall
Excessive oiling can clog follicles and worsen scalp conditions — medical treatment is needed for real causes
Cutting hair makes it grow back thicker
Hair thickness is determined by follicle health — trimming has zero effect on regrowth or density
Hair loss is permanent once it starts
Most types of hair loss are treatable — especially when diagnosed early with the right medical approach
Shampoo and supplements are enough to fix hair loss
Without treating the root cause (DHT, deficiency, hormones), no shampoo or supplement can reverse hair loss
Only men suffer from significant hair loss
40% of women experience visible hair thinning by age 40 — female pattern hair loss is widely underdiagnosed
Stress-related hair loss is temporary and self-resolves
Chronic stress-induced hair loss (Telogen Effluvium) can become permanent without proper treatment
